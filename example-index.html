I really think that Ferrari are in the running to win both the constructors and drivers championship. Both Sainz and Lecrec are really excellent drivers, and the new car is really fast (both Red Bull and Mercedes have conceded that point). However, let's not forget about Mercedes and Redbull, because both teams are in it to win and both teams are hungry to claim the other's awards. Coming off of Pre-season testing though it doesn't look so good for Mercedes, since it seems that their "zero-pod" gamble might not actually be a game changer like their engine and aero design was for the 2014 rule changes, which cemented their current dominance, but they might be doing what they've always liked to do: Sandbagging. Max is fast, but his teammate continues to be a heartache, because it seems that my countryman isn't to the same level as he should be if Redbull want to win the Constructors Championship. Haas and Williams seem to be improing, the latter more so after changing lead principals to someone who is more experienced and with a pedigree and rally mindset that can potentially bring Williams back to the top. Haas might actually score points after bringing on Kevin Magnussen, who did fairly well in IMSA SportsCar Championship. Aston Martin is my underdog team, and I hope that Vettel regains his spirits and puts on a great show this season.
